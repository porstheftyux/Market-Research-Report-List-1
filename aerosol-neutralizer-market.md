<p><p>The aerosol neutralizer market is divided into two main types: radioactive neutralizers and non-radioactive neutralizers. Radioactive neutralizers employ isotopes to neutralize airborne particles, often used in specialized industrial applications where high efficacy is required. Non-radioactive neutralizers, on the other hand, utilize chemical or physical methods, such as filters or ionization, to mitigate aerosol pollution in various environments, including indoor air quality systems and environmental control. Both types aim to enhance air safety and improve health outcomes.</p></p>
